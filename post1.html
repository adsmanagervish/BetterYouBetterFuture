Growing Up in Indian Society — And Why I Started Questioning Everything

By Vish | Better You Better Future

I still remember the day my cousin was told to learn cooking because "that's what girls do." She was 12. She wanted to be an engineer. Nobody asked her what she wanted.

And I remember sitting there, quietly thinking — why does nobody question this?

"We were all handed a script the moment we were born. A script that told us how to behave, what to feel, and who to become — based on nothing but our gender."

🎭 The Role Nobody Auditioned For

Growing up in India is beautiful in many ways — the warmth, the festivals, the sense of community. But underneath all of that, there is a quiet pressure that most of us never talk about.

Boys are told — don't cry, be strong, provide. Girls are told — be quiet, be adjusting, be sacrificing. And anyone who doesn't fit neatly into these boxes? They are made to feel like something is wrong with them.

Nobody chooses these roles. But almost everyone plays them. Because questioning them feels dangerous. It feels like betraying your family. Like being ungrateful.

💡 The Moment I Started Questioning

For me, the shift happened slowly. I started noticing small things. Who always served the food at family gatherings. Who was asked about their career and who was asked about their marriage. Who was allowed to be angry and who was told to smile.

And once I started noticing — I couldn't stop.

I wasn't angry at my family. They were just doing what they were taught. But I realized that just because something is old, doesn't mean it's right. And just because everyone does it, doesn't mean we have to.

🌱 5 Small Ways to Start Questioning Old Traditions

  • Notice before you react. Start paying attention to gender roles in your daily life — at home, in conversations, in media. Awareness is always the first step.
  • Ask "why" gently. When you see an old tradition that doesn't feel right, ask why it exists. Not aggressively — just curiously. "Why do only women cook at festivals?" is a powerful question.
  • Have one honest conversation. Pick one person you trust — a friend, a sibling. Talk about something you've noticed. You'll be surprised how many people feel the same way but never said it out loud.
  • Read and expand your mind. Books like Men Explain Things to Me or The God of Small Things can open your eyes to things you never noticed before.
  • Give yourself permission to be different. You don't have to follow every tradition blindly. Choosing what to keep and what to leave behind is not disrespect — it's growth.

💛 You Are Not Alone

If you've ever felt trapped by expectations you never agreed to — this blog is for you. You are not too sensitive. You are not overreacting. You are just awake.

And being awake is the beginning of everything. 🌱

— Vish
