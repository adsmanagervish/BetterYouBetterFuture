The Pressure of Being a "Good Indian Child" — And How to Finally Breathe

By Vish | Better You Better Future

When I was growing up, there was a plan already made for me. Not by me — but for me. A career path quietly decided at the dinner table. A future drawn out before I even knew what I wanted.

And the scariest part? I almost followed it. Not because I wanted to — but because I didn't want to disappoint anyone.

"Being a good Indian child often means carrying everyone else's dreams — while quietly burying your own."

🎒 The Weight We Carry

In Indian families, love and pressure often come packaged together. Your parents work hard, sacrifice a lot, and want the best for you. That part is real and beautiful.

But somewhere along the way, "wanting the best for you" becomes "deciding what's best for you." And suddenly you're studying engineering when you wanted to paint. Or becoming a doctor when you wanted to write. Or sitting in an office when your heart was somewhere else entirely.

Nobody meant to hurt you. But here you are — living someone else's life. And wondering why you feel so empty inside. 💔

😶 Why We Stay Silent

Speaking up feels impossible because the stakes feel so high. You think —

"If I say what I really want, they'll think I'm ungrateful."

"If I choose my own path, I'll be letting everyone down."

"Maybe they know better than me. Maybe I'm being selfish."

So you stay quiet. You smile. You perform the role of the good child. And deep inside, a small voice keeps asking — but what about me?

"Choosing yourself is not selfishness. It is the most honest thing you can do — for yourself and for the people who love you."

💡 The Shift I Had to Make

The turning point for me was realizing something simple but powerful — my life is not a gift I give to my parents. It is mine to live.

That doesn't mean I stopped caring about my family. It means I started caring about myself too. It means I understood that a child who lives someone else's dream doesn't become happy — they become resentful. And that helps nobody.

🌱 5 Ways to Give Yourself Permission to Choose

  • Separate love from control. Your parents loving you and your parents controlling your future are two different things. You can receive one and gently push back on the other.
  • Write down what YOU actually want. Not what sounds responsible. Not what your family expects. What do YOU want your life to look like? A journal is a great place to start this conversation with yourself. 📓
  • Start with small choices. You don't have to make one big dramatic decision. Start by choosing small things — what you wear, what you read, how you spend one free hour. Practice choosing yourself in small ways every day.
  • Find one person who gets it. A friend, a cousin, a mentor — someone who understands the pressure you feel. You don't have to carry this alone.
  • Remember — your happiness matters. A happy, fulfilled child brings more joy to a family than a successful but miserable one. Choosing yourself is not betrayal. It is courage. 💪

💛 To Every Young Person Reading This

You are not a trophy to be displayed. You are not a retirement plan. You are not anyone's second chance at their own dreams.

You are a whole, complete human being with your own desires, your own strengths, and your own path to walk.

And you have every right to walk it. 🌱

— Vish
